Malabar Trogon · Watercolour

From feather
to thread

The Malabar Trogon — with its striking contrast of deep crimson, velvety black, amber, and electric blue — became the colour vocabulary for this project.

Beginning with an illustration of the bird, colours were extracted and translated into stripe palettes. These palettes were then woven as samples, exploring how different weft sequences alter the rhythm and mood of the same colour set.

The final outcome is a stole — a wearable textile art where the Trogon's plumage lives on in every thread crossing.
